The Team

Meet Our Researchers

A/Prof Rachael Dangarembizi

Principal Investigator

An Associate Professor in Neuroscience, her work focuses on cryptococcal brain invasion, lesion development, and host–pathogen interactions. She leads the lab’s translational neuroimmunology programme, integrating in vivo models, deep-tissue imaging, and molecular profiling to uncover mechanisms of brain pathology.

rachael.dangarembizi@uct.ac.za

Nawaal Samsodien

Lab Manager

Oversees laboratory operations, training, and research coordination. She specialises in maintaining high-containment fungal models, optimising experimental workflows, and ensuring reproducibility across imaging and molecular pipelines.

nawaal.samsodien@uct.ac.za

Dr Amalia Awala

Postdoctoral Researcher

A postdoctoral scientist investigating immune regulation within cryptococcal lesions. Her research bridges immunophenotyping, cytokine mapping, and microenvironmental analysis to understand how host responses shape fungal persistence.

amalia.awala@uct.ac.za

Dr Anja de Lange

Postdoctoral Researcher

Focuses on computational neurobiology and advanced 3D microscopy. Her work involves developing image-analysis pipelines for quantifying lesion structure, oxygen gradients, and spatial immune interactions using high-resolution datasets.

anja.de.lange@uct.ac.za

Maahir Kauchali

PhD Student

Studies metabolic adaptation of Cryptococcus in hypoxic brain niches. His research integrates fungal physiology, metabolic assays, and in vivo modelling to characterise the energetic strategies that support long-term survival.

maahir.kauchali@uct.ac.za

Simran Patel

PhD Student

A doctoral candidate investigating neuroinflammatory signalling during cryptococcal infection. Her work combines molecular assays, confocal imaging, and transcriptomic profiling to dissect the pathways governing host defence.

simran.patel@uct.ac.za

Yanga Pato

PhD Student

A doctoral researcher studying early cryptococcal lesion formation using in vivo infection models and organotypic slice cultures. Her work focuses on defining the cellular events and immune signalling pathways that shape initial brain colonisation, integrating imaging workflows with quantitative analysis.

yanga.pato@uct.ac.za

Lilitha Cengani

Master's Student

Investigates microglial responses to cryptococcal lesions using slice cultures and quantitative image analysis. Her work explores how immune cells reshape local tissue structure and influence lesion progression.

lilitha.cengani@uct.ac.za

Saba Gebreseilassie

Master's Student

Focuses on deep-tissue hypoxia profiling and the development of automated analysis pipelines. Her project maps metabolic and structural heterogeneity within cryptococcal lesions using Fiji macros, computational workflows, and confocal imaging.

saba.gebreseilassie@uct.ac.za

Blessing Gumbu

Master's Student

Explores host–fungal interactions using organotypic slice models. His research aims to characterise early lesion formation and the cellular mechanisms that contribute to fungal containment or escape.

blessing.gumbu@uct.ac.za

Masilo Matlakala

Master's Student

Works on lesion oxygenation and immune microenvironment mapping. His project uses pimonidazole staining, confocal microscopy, and spatial analysis to quantify hypoxia-driven shifts in fungal and host behaviour.

masilo.matlakala@uct.ac.za

The Crypto Lab studies lesion biology, host–pathogen interactions, and hypoxia-driven microenvironments in Cryptococcus neoformans infections.
